Building a Rhetorical Situation Using Music

What is a Rhetorical Situation?

Something is a rhetorical activity, event, or situation when it's...

  • Shaped by language and communication.
    • Music is the form of communication!
  • Used to persuade people to do something
    • Examples:
      • To promote awareness for a social issue.
      • To allow a more personal look into rhetor's life.
      • Make people feel a certain emotion.
    • When writing music you draw from the Rhetorical Constituents
      • Exigence, Goal, Audience, Rhetors, Constraints, Mode/Technology, Context, and Genre.

Musical Preference & Personality

Studies show that there is significant correlation between preferences for key and tempo with certain traits.

  • Major Key and Fast Tempo
    • Preference correlated to traits like optimism, emotional stability, conscientiousness and agreeableness.
  • Minor Key and Slow Tempo
    • Preference of this kind of music was shown to correlate to "openness" to new experiences.
      • The definition of "openness" was described as imagination, curiosity, creativity, awareness of oneâ€™s own emotions, and introversion.
        • Introverts preference for minor key and slow tempo could be correlated to their constant state of high arousal level and the low arousal levels of such music.
        • Level of arousal meaning the degree of alertness, responsiveness, stress, and level of distraction.

Musical Preference & Emotions

In a study music was manipulated to vary in tempo and mode/key.

  • People were asked what emotion they perceived from the music and how they felt from the music.
    • The study found a correlation between what the perceived emotion and the emotions they felt.
    • High tempo and major key were considered to be happy music while low tempo and minor key were considered to be sad music
      • Perception ratings were higher than feeling ratings for both "sad" and "happy" music
  • Musical preference changes based on age! (Audience)
    • Older adults, compared to younger adults, are more likely to attend to pleasant situations and avoid unpleasant ones.

Music and Speech

  • Music does not only impact us the way a rhetor wants.
  • Speech and music are tied together.
    • Speech does not rely on pitch alone, but nonetheless the speech signal includes important pitch-based information.
    • Particularly in a study it was shown that...
      • Children who received musical training showed enhanced responses to pitch variations in music and in speech.
      • They also showed greater improvements on a behavioral reading task
    • This shows that we may apply some ideas/emotions from generic pitch changes in our speech to exaggerated changes in music.
